Plumbing FAQs: Choosing the right tradie & getting quality work

How do I check a plumber is licensed in South Australia?

Ask to see their digital or plastic licence and cross-check the number on the SA Consumer and Business Services register before any work starts. Licensed plumbers must meet minimum standards and insurance requirements.

What should a written quote include?

Request a fixed or itemised estimate covering labour, materials, call-out fees, after-hours rates, and any potential extras (digging, concrete cutting, permits). Avoid verbal-only pricing.

Which questions help me compare plumbers?

Ask: Who will do the work and are they licensed? Have you done similar jobs in Mount Barker homes? Do you handle council permits? What warranties apply to labour and parts? How soon can you attend and finish?

What red flags should I avoid?

Extremely low quotes with vague scope, pressure to decide on the spot, reluctance to show licence/insurance, insistence on large upfront payment, or no written agreement. Search recent reviews for the business name and phone number to spot aliases.

How can I make sure the job is done properly?

Get before/after photos and CCTV footage for drain work, keep copies of invoices and warranties, and confirm the plumber will clean up and dispose of waste. If it’s a big job, schedule a walk-through before final payment.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Yes—aim for three quotes from local, licensed plumbers to benchmark price and approach. It also protects against inflated emergency-callout fees.
