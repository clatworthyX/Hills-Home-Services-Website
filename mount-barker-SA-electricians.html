Electrician FAQs: choosing well & staying safe

How do I check an electrician is licensed in SA?

Ask for their licence number and verify it on the SA Consumer and Business Services register. Only licensed electricians can legally perform electrical work.

What should be in a quote?

Look for a clear scope, fixed or itemised pricing, parts brands, after-hours rates, and warranties on labour and materials. Avoid verbal-only quotes.

Which questions help me choose?

Ask: Who will attend and are they licensed? Do you provide a Certificate of Compliance? Have you worked on similar homes in Mount Barker? When can you start and finish?

What red flags should I avoid?

Reluctance to show licence/insurance, ultra-low prices without detail, pressure for cash up-front, or no written agreement. Search recent reviews for their business name and phone number.

How do I make sure the job is safe?

Request RCD testing, smoke-alarm checks and a Certificate of Compliance after work. Keep photos and receipts, and test safety switches monthly.

Do I need multiple quotes?

Yes—three quotes from local licensed electricians help benchmark price, availability and approach, especially for switchboard or rewiring work.
